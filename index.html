Preparation

What is the Vratam

  • A simple, powerful puja to Lord Sri Venkateswara — open to anyone, any faith.
  • Two ways: the detailed method (this course) and a simple 20-minute method (read the 5 stories). Equal fruit to both.
  • The promise: performed with faith, the Lord Himself comes — as a guest, an animal, a bird — to take the prasad.
  • No fasting, no rigid rules. Purity of mind is what matters.
Talk-track — The Sri Venkateswara Vratam is one of the easiest and most beloved ways to seek the grace of Lord Srinivasa. It asks for nothing but faith and a pure mind. You may perform it in the morning or evening, at home, in a temple, or on a riverbank. In this course we walk the detailed method step by step.

Preparation

Setup — Mandapa, Kalasam, Materials

  • Clean the puja area thoroughly.
  • Set up a mandapam; place the photo of Kalyana Venkateswara with Sridevi & Bhudevi (or the Lord alone / a small idol / a one-rupee coin).
  • Arrange a Kalasam (water pot).
  • Keep ready: tulasi, coconut, fruits, flowers, jaggery, panchamrutham (milk, curd, ghee, honey, sugar), haldi & kumkum, akshata, incense, lamp.
  • Invite family & friends — all who attend and take prasad share the blessing.
Talk-track — A clean place invites a clean mind. Place the Lord's picture — ideally Kalyana Venkateswara with His consorts. If you don't have it, His image alone, a small idol, or even a coin to stand for Him is enough; the Lord looks at the heart. Place the Kalasam to the right. The Lord is especially fond of tulasi, so be sure to keep it.

  • Use a separate vessel; fill the Kalasam with water; place it to the Lord's right.
  • Add gandham, kumkum, akshata and a flower; cover with your hand and recite.
  • Add tulasi; invoke the holy rivers into the water.
  • Sprinkle the consecrated water on the Lord, the puja items, and yourself.

2 · Sri Viswamitra Uvaacha

  • The poor scholar Vishnuchittha and his devout wife Thaaraamathi, worried for their nine daughters' marriages.
  • An old Brahmin teaches them the Vratam; they perform it. He vanishes overnight, leaving the Lord's idol — and their home is filled with gold.
  • Lesson: the Lord Himself comes as a guest to those who perform the Vratam with devotion.

3 · Sri Vasishta Uvaacha

  • The proud, godless rich of Bhagyanagaram are humbled when a flood washes away their wealth on Tholi Ekadasi.

5 · Sri Athri Uvaacha

  • The righteous, disabled washerman Mithrudu is taught the Vratam by an old Brahmin (the Lord), on a promise to share half the gold.
  • He gains a royal post and a thousand coins — but forgets his promise, and suffers false imprisonment until he repents.
  • The Lord pardons him; he keeps the Vratam every month for life and attains mukti.

Conclusion

Kshamaarpanam · Theertham · Udvaasanam

  • Kshamaarpanam — ask the Lord's forgiveness for any error or lack in the worship.
  • Take Theertham — drink the holy water.
  • Offer the final Mahaa Naivedyam; then Thaamboolam, Mantrapushpam, Namaskaaram once more.
  • Udvaasanam — respectfully conclude, asking the Lord to remain in His abode.
  • Distribute prasadam & blessed tulasi; break and share the coconut.
